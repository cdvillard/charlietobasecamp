Hey Basecamp!

My name is Charles. I'm a graphic designer with some marketing background under my belt. Weary of the office grind, I started checking We Work Remotely several times a day looking for the right opportunity. I never thought that opportunity would be to work with all of these cool people at Basecamp. >>

I am a fan of Basecamp, but not because I use products daily. I haven't had that pleasure yet. I am a fan of your philosophies surrounding design and the work environment. Until recently, I checked We Work Remotely almost daily just for the heck of it. I read Signal vs. Noise whenever I need a spark of inspiration. Your debate against Matthew Stillman on remote working was simply brilliant and hugely convincing, and I'm thinking of approaching Ruby and Ruby on Rails as the next tools in my growing web development tool box.

I don't use Basecamp.
So why me?

I fit the bill.

Let's just get this boring part out of the way. I fill the requirements listed as per your solicitation. I worked in design and marketing right out of college, and have dabbled in both the print and digital spaces. I originally wanted to be a copy writer, but design sucked me in before I could get too many one-liners out. I can still write some tight copy, but my heart lies with pixels and picas.

I'm well-rounded.

In my time, I've worn the hats of designer, marketer, copy writer, proposal writer, cartoonist, photographer, translation technologist, and a few more that don't come to mind right now. To be frank, I'm not afraid to draw up that comp for you, but I don't mind getting my hands muddy with other things either.

I want to learn more.

I've worked for a lot of great companies in my time, but never really got the chance to grow professionally. Currently, I work for the Miami Herald Media Company here in Miami, Florida. They're a great bunch of people, a local institution that's taught me a lot about newsprint, but they don't fit into my long-term goals.

As of last year, I started learning web development for fun and profit, and while I've had the chance to do a bit of markup for them, I can't say it was a learning experience. But what better place for someone to learn more about the world of the web than Basecamp, the home of Ruby on Rails? After reading about what Mig learned in the first month, how could I not be enticed?

I'm not a shy guy.

I don't mind getting out from behind the screen to talk to people face-to-face. I've represented companies and organizations at a number of events and conventions in the past, and have worked as an entertainer for an amazing non-profit stage combat troupe called Urban Ronin. I enjoy connecting with people, whether it be peers at a professional conference or children from on stage.
